## 🚀 10. Weather Observation Station 4

### 💡 Idea

We need to find the **difference between the total number of city entries and the number of distinct city names** in the `STATION` table.

The `COUNT()` function counts all records, while `COUNT(DISTINCT CITY)` counts only unique city names.

---

### 💻 Solution

```sql
SELECT COUNT(CITY) - COUNT(DISTINCT CITY)
FROM STATION;
```

---

### 🛠️ How It Works

1. `COUNT(CITY)` counts every non-NULL city entry in the table.
2. `COUNT(DISTINCT CITY)` counts each unique city only once.
3. Subtract the number of unique cities from the total number of city entries.
4. The result represents the number of duplicate city records.

---

### ⏱️ Complexity

**Time Complexity:** `O(n)` *(scans all rows in the `STATION` table)*

**Space Complexity:** `O(n)` *(used internally for tracking distinct city names)*
